Your Right to Be Forgotten

About Data Deletion

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to request the deletion of your personal data from our systems. This is also known as the "right to erasure" or "right to be forgotten."

What Data Can Be Deleted

Upon your request, we can delete:

  • Personal contact information (name, email, phone)
  • Account and profile data
  • Communication history
  • Usage and analytics data
  • Marketing preferences and history
  • Any other personal data we hold about you

Data We May Need to Retain

Please note that we may be required to retain certain data for legal compliance:

  • Financial records (7 years for tax purposes)
  • Contract-related documents
  • Data necessary for legal claims or disputes
  • Data required by law enforcement

In such cases, we will delete all data not subject to retention requirements and inform you of what must be retained and why.

How to Request Data Deletion

Method 1: Email Request

Send your deletion request to:

privacy@upsellbusinessagency.com

Include:

  • Your full name
  • Email address associated with our services
  • Specific data you want deleted (or "all data")
  • Reason for deletion (optional)

Method 2: Online Form

What Happens Next

1

Request Received

We acknowledge your request within 48 hours

2

Identity Verification

3

We identify all data associated with you

4

Deletion Process

Your data is permanently deleted from our systems

5

Confirmation

You receive confirmation of deletion within 30 days

Important Notes

⚠️ Warning: Data deletion is permanent and cannot be reversed. This includes:

  • Loss of access to any services or accounts
  • Deletion of project history and communications
  • Removal from all mailing lists
  • Loss of any stored preferences or settings

Alternative Options

Before requesting full deletion, consider these alternatives:

  • Data Export: Request a copy of your data first
  • Unsubscribe: Opt-out of marketing communications only
  • Data Correction: Update incorrect information
  • Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
